Likes

1. Job Security: TCS is known for providing excellent job stability. Unlike many private companies that often downsize during market changes, TCS follows a more people-friendly approach. They invest in long-term careers, which makes employees feel secure even during tough economic conditions. 2. Skill Development: TCS offers a wide range of learning and training opportunities. From online courses to certifications and workshops, employees can constantly upgrade their technical and professional skills. This helps them stay relevant in the IT industry and grow within the company or even outside if needed. 3. Mini Government Job: TCS is often referred to as a "mini government job" because of its structured work environment, job security, timely salary, good work-life balance, and employee benefits like PF, insurance, and leave policies. It gives a stable, low-risk career feeling similar to a government job but with a corporate touch. 4. Strict Security Compliance: TCS follows very strictsecurity protocols to protect client and company data. Whether it's about physical office access, data privacy, or IT systems, TCS ensures compliance with international standards like ISO and GDPR. This creates a trustworthy and professional work culture....read more

Dislikes

1. No Recognition for Work: Despite working hard and meeting targets, many employees feel that their efforts often go unnoticed. Recognition and rewards are limited, and promotions can be slow even for high performers, which can demotivate employees over time. 2. No Food Card or Meal Benefits: Unlike some other top IT companies, TCS doesn’t offer a food card or daily meal allowance. This can add to monthly expenses, especially for employees working from offices where food options are costly. 3. Bad Office Politics: Workplace politics can sometimes interfere with career growth. Internal favoritism, biased team leads, or managers promoting only close associates are common complaints in certain projects or departments. 4. Gender Bias: While TCS promotes diversity on paper, some employees feel there is still an underlying gender bias in leadership roles or team dynamics. Women may not get equal opportunities for growth or challenging projects in some cases. 5. Traffic Issues (Long Commute): Many TCS offices are located in tech parks or outskirts of major cities. Employees often face long commuting hours due to traffic, especially in cities like Bangalore, Mumbai, or Hyderabad. This affects work-life balance. 6. Rigid Policies: TCS maintains strict rules and policies. While that ensures discipline, it also limits flexibility. For example, getting leave approvals, remote work flexibility, or project transfers can sometimes be very difficult. 7. Average Salary Increments: Salary hikes at TCS are generally lower than the industry standard, especially for long-term employees. Even after yearly appraisals, many feel their compensation doesn’t match their contribution or market value. 8. Old Technologies in Some Projects: Not all projects use the latest tech stacks. Some employees may get stuck with legacy technologies for years, which can slow down their career growth in the fast-changing IT industry. 9. Limited Onsite Opportunities: While TCS has a global presence, getting onsite opportunities is very competitive. Many employees spend years waiting without any chance to go abroad due to strict project/client approvals....read more
